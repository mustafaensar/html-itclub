MERCEDES-BENZ 280 SE 3.5 COUPE (W 111)

In May 1971 the production of the 2.8-liter coupés and convertible ended and two months later the production of the 8-cylinder versions also ceased, thus bringing the ten-year era of the 111 and 112 series coupés and convertible to an end. A total of 28,918 coupés and 7,013 convertible of this range were built in Sindelfingen.The 350 SLC, which became available from February 1972, was the successor of the coupé models. This car was not based on a saloon, however, but on the SL model, which had been launched in April 1971. No replacement was planned for the convertibles; fresh air Mercedes drivers had to be content with the two-seater SL.
